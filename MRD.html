Initiative: Preventative Maintenance (PM) Scheduler

Overview: Trimble Unity Maintain currently encounters a strategic expectation gap when compared to enterprise incumbents. The absence of a centralized, asset-based preventative maintenance (PM) scheduling engine is not merely a functional omission but a significant commercial vulnerability. This gap has already accounted for more than $1 million in lost revenue from major accounts such as LA Sanitation, and puts an additional $925,000 in Annual Recurring Revenue (ARR) at immediate risk.2 The strategic objective of this initiative, therefore, is to deliver a next-generation AI-driven scheduling engine that eliminates "dark data" silos and automates complex nesting logic, thereby positioning Trimble Unity Maintain as the premier GIS-centric platform for "careful adopters" who must modernize without risking service continuity.

Currently, agencies are challenged with wasted effort and money due to reactive or "run to failure" maintenance. Product Requirements (MVP)

Overview: Based on the synthesized market gaps, this section outlines the core functionality required for the Minimum Viable Product (MVP) of the PM Scheduler. These capabilities focus on shifting from single-work-order recurrence to holistic, asset-based schedule management, fulfilling the "Jobs To Be Done" framework identified in internal workshops.

1

Centralized Schedule Creation

  • Asset-Centric: Create schedules directly tied to an asset or group of assets ("Smart Lists" via GIS criteria).
  • Templates: Select Work Activity Templates and customize ELM (Equipment, Labor, Material) specifics for that schedule.
  • Recurrence: Support complex patterns (e.g., specific days of the week, seasonal dates) without needing multiple schedules.
2

Advanced Trigger Logic

  • Time & Usage: Trigger based on time intervals, usage thresholds (odometer, hours), or "whichever comes first" logic.
  • Condition-Based: Trigger based on asset condition score thresholds or telemetry behaviors (vibration, SCADA).
  • Buffer Zones: Define a "Lead Time" or "Days Ahead" buffer to generate work orders just-in-time, reducing inbox clutter.
3

Nesting & Hierarchy

Prevent duplicate work by building hierarchical rules. If a major overhaul is due, skip the minor inspections.

├─ Annual Overhaul (Highest Priority)
│  └─ Suppresses: Quarterly Inspection
│  └─ Suppresses: Monthly Lube
4

PM Command Center

  • Dashboard: Manage all schedules from a single pane. View overdue PM work.
  • Ghost Records: Provide a calendar view forecasting labor and material costs (1, 2, 5-year projections) using unsaved simulation records.
  • Migration: Provide a smooth upgrade path for legacy schedules into the new engine.

AI-Enhanced Analysis & Future Vision

Overview: While the MVP focuses on establishing a robust rules-based engine, Phase 2 will leverage AI to move customers from static compliance to risk-adjusted Prescriptive Maintenance. This section visualizes the impact of the proposed Time Series Foundation Model (TSFM) which analyzes historical "dark data" to dynamically adjust maintenance priority.

The Dark Data Opportunity

From Age to Risk Intelligence

Traditional scheduling relies on static OEM manuals (e.g., "replace every 5 years"). TSFM treats every field event—repairs, usage spikes, unstructured technician notes like "excessive vibration noticed"—as variables influencing the asset's trajectory toward failure.

  • ✓ The Digital Auditor: Agentic AI scans notes to suggest shortening PM intervals before catastrophic failure.
  • ✓ Proximity Planning: Algorithms group geospatially close tasks to reduce mobilization costs.

[Sources: Deep Research: AI Enhancements; TSFMs for Maintenance Prioritization]

Deterioration Modeling: Static Age vs. TSFM AI Risk Score

The TSFM recognizes non-linear degradation. A minor repair drops the risk temporarily, but the underlying curve accelerates over time based on historical failure patterns.

🚀 The Strategic Shift

By integrating TSFM into the PM Scheduler, the core value proposition of Trimble Unity Maintain evolves from simple Asset Tracking to Optimized Capital Preservation. We empower users to answer the crucial question: "Which deferred PM work order, if completed today, provides the largest reduction in system-wide risk?"

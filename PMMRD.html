Section Context: Translating insights into action, this section details the functional pillars of the MVP (Minimum Viable Product). It defines the specific capabilities needed to build a "Command Center" that solves the JTBD struggles, focusing on asset-centricity, nesting, and flexible triggers.

🏢

1. Centralized Asset Scheduler

  • ✔️ Asset-Centric View: A single pane to answer "What are all the schedules for this specific asset?"
  • ✔️ Smart Groups: Schedule by dynamic GIS criteria (e.g., "All hydrants in Zone 4") rather than static lists.
  • ✔️ Templates: Select Work Activity Templates and customize ELM (Equipment, Labor, Material) per schedule.
⚙️

2. Advanced Trigger Logic

  • ✔️ Whichever Comes First: Combine time intervals (e.g., 6 months) and usage thresholds (e.g., 500 hours).
  • ✔️ Condition-Based: Trigger based on asset condition score or telemetry (SCADA integration).
  • ✔️ Lead Time Buffers: Define "Days Ahead" to generate work orders just-in-time for planning, reducing inbox clutter.
📂

3. Nesting & Hierarchy

Prevent duplicate work by building hierarchical rules to suppress minor PMs when major overhauls occur.

IF "Annual Overhaul" IS DUE
  THEN SUPPRESS "Quarterly Inspection"
  THEN SUPPRESS "Monthly Lube"
RESULT: 1 Consolidated Work Order
📊

4. PM Command Center

  • ✔️ Unified Dashboard: Manage all schedules. View overdue PM work easily.
  • ✔️ Forecasting: Provide a calendar view forecasting labor/material costs using unsaved "Ghost Records".
  • ✔️ Migration Path: Smooth upgrade tooling for legacy schedules.

Section Context: Looking beyond the MVP, this section outlines the Phase 2 strategy: utilizing a Time Series Foundation Model (TSFM) to shift customers from rigid compliance to Prescriptive Maintenance. It visualizes how AI analyzes historical "dark data" to dynamically adjust priority, ensuring labor is spent where risk is highest.

The "Dark Data" Opportunity

Traditional scheduling relies on static OEM manuals. TSFMs treat every field event (repairs, usage spikes, unstructured notes) as variables influencing the asset's trajectory toward failure.

🤖 The Digital Auditor

Agentic AI scans unstructured technician notes (e.g., "excessive vibration noticed") to suggest shortening PM intervals before catastrophic failure.

📍 Proximity Planning

Algorithms group geospatially close tasks to reduce mobilization costs, turning a calendar list into an optimized route.

Deterioration Modeling: Static Age vs. TSFM Risk Score

The TSFM recognizes non-linear degradation. A minor repair drops the risk temporarily, but the underlying curve accelerates over time based on historical patterns.

The Strategic Shift

By integrating TSFM into the PM Scheduler, the core value proposition of Unity Maintain evolves from simple Asset Tracking to Optimized Capital Preservation. We empower users to answer the crucial question:

"Which deferred PM work order, if completed today, provides the largest reduction in system-wide risk?"

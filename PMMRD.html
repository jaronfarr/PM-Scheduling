The MVP is focused on consolidating separate admin screens into a single, cohesive workflow. This includes the implementation of "Nesting" and "Usage Thresholds" which were identified as non-negotiable for large-scale implementations.

1. Centralized UI

  • ✓
    Unified View: "Asset Record Scheduler" tool. One place to see: "What are the schedules for this asset?"
  • ✓
    Smart Groups: Schedule by GIS criteria (e.g., "All hydrants in Zone 4") rather than static lists.

2. Nesting Logic

IF "Major Overhaul" (Annually) IS DUE
  THEN SKIP "Monthly Inspection"
  THEN SKIP "Weekly Lube"
  RESULT: 1 Consolidated WO

Why this matters:

Stops "never-ending" loops and duplicates identified in implementer feedback (Elisa Peters, WSP).

3. Trigger Resilience

  • ✓
    Lead-Time Buffers: Allow work orders to trigger X days before they are due for planning.
  • ✓
    Usage Sync: Connect to SCADA/Odometer data for "Whichever comes first" intervals.

4. Forecasting Engine

Projected labor/material load based on "Ghost Records".

The Time Series Foundation Model (TSFM) represents Phase 2. It treats every event (failures, repairs, usage spikes) as variables influencing the asset's trajectory. This shifts EAM from simple "Asset Tracking" to "Optimized Capital Preservation."

TSFM: Modeling Non-Linear Deterioration

The TSFM recognizes that a 15-year-old pipe might deteriorate 5x faster in year 16 than it did in year 5.
Outcome: Dynamic priority scoring based on historical failure patterns (the "Target").

🔎 The Digital Auditor

An AI agent scans technician notes (e.g., "excessive vibration noticed") to suggest shortening PM intervals automatically.

📍 Proximity Planning

Geospatial grouping of tasks to reduce mobilization costs (e.g., "all grease traps within 50ft of this restaurant").

📊 ROI Potential

Moves from "Run-to-Failure" to a reduction in total lifecycle costs by up to 15% through precision timing.
